Urban Burn Stockholm 2017 is the very bare bones version of a participatory festival, inspired by Burning Man. By buying a ticket, all you get is access to an empty room. If no one comes along and creates something, it will stay empty for the duration of the event. Everything, including security, ticketing, fire safety and even this document, is created by all of us.
This is an experiment in creative freedom and participation, conceived, built, experienced and returned by nothing but its participants. Urban Burn is a non-profit event with no consumers, only co-creators.

We start with 2000 sqm of empty open indoor space, we fill it with art, installations, love and awesomeness and then we empty it again. It’s magic, created by our communal effort and imagination.

It’s like a giant house party… but where everyone brings their own furniture.

(and art, and workshop, and fun clothes, and whatever else they need to have fun)

There are no limits to our imagination at Urban Burn. The project could be an open stage for improvised performances, a silent meditation area, an interactive art project, a table and a couple of chairs to drink tea with a stranger, or a giant piano to walk on. It will be anything we want it to be.

Once all the projects will be installed, Urban Burn will culminate in two days of awesome celebrations, with a festive event aimed at enjoying the art, the beauty, the energy, and the music born by our magic encounter. The last day, following the LNT (Leave No Trace) policy, the place will be cleaned by the participants and left as we found it. The only permanent change will happen within each one of us.

FAQ

What will I get for the ticket?

  • Access to a big empty playground.
  • Water, toilets, power, security.
  • The opportunity to apply for art grants.

That is all. Everything else, including, but not limited to, food, extension chords, sound system, shelter, candy, art, hugs, clothing, music, dreams, medical supplies, fire safety, common sense, cleaning, loving and dancing will be up to the participants of the Burn to provide.

Do I need to be a Burner, Borderling or have previous experience with The Borderland or Burning Man to attend Urban Burn 2017?

Chances are that you heard about this event from friends or friends of friends that might be part of the Borderland or Burning Man communities, if so then you probably know that we are all about inviting everyone from any corner of society in every corner of the word  into our collaborative gatherings. So, the answer is No, you do not need to have any previous experience in any other Burning Man inspired event! :) and the good news is that this can be your very first one!  If you feel intrigued, curious and fascinated by expressing and co-creating in a space where you will give that very unique part of your vision of life to many other co-creators, then why wait? Join this fantastic and Ecletical Spectacle.

How much do I have to work?

So, what does it mean to "participate", to contribute? Well, only you can be the judge of your own self-expression. Whether you build giant contraptions, play music, make smoothies or give excellent massages, you do it on your own conditions. It is up to you to find a way of contributing that you are comfortable with. Along your way, you will find many who are willing to help you, and many who will be needing help. Participation is not about performance, it's a way of looking at the world around you as an opportunity to give.

Can I play music/DJ at Urban Burn?

Yes, if you bring a sound system or find someone who will bring a sound system. As the venue will be fairly crowded, with a lot of different music, performances and moods, there will be some restrictions on how loud you can play.

How about alcohol?

Due to Swedish laws, the only alcohol allowed will be sold by the venue. The bar will be open 18.00-03.00 on Friday and Saturday night. Do not bring your own alcohol to the event. Please note that money in other forms are very uninteresting - alcohol is the only thing that will be traded for money at this event and everything else is unconditional gifting.

Can I bring my kids to the Urban Burn?

Children and teenagers under the age of 18 can come to Urban Burn when there is no alcohol being sold. This means you can bring them on the 12th, 13th, and during daytime on the 14th and 15th. Children enter free of charge if accompanied by a parent or caretaker.

Who cleans up? What is this “Leave no Trace”-thing?

Leaving No Trace means being mindful of the trash - or "Matter Out Of Place" (MOOP) - we create around us. Think of this as being your best friend's house party. If you realize you have trash in your hand, put it in a trash can instead of leaving it on a table. If you have a bottle, recycle it immediately when it gets empty, instead of postponing it until another time.

The Sunday, March 13, is dedicated to dismounting and removing any projects built during the week and weekend. You are responsible for removing anything that you brought to the location and leave as it was. Some recycling will be provided, but if you bring bigger stuff, you need to have a way to get it back out.

How do I bring stuff to Urban Burn?

You can drive straight into the venue at all times except 20.00-03.00 on Friday and Saturday night (then you have to unload on the parking lot next to the venue). If you need help with transport, please ask around in #logistics on Slack.

Will there be a place to check in clothing or valuables?

There will only be an unmanned coat check / storage. Neither Nobelberget or Urban Burn will take responsibility for any items lost during the event.

It's only April! How cold is it going to be?

It's indoors, and we have some heating, but it's not very warm. If you're not dancing you might want to keep a good sweater or a jacket on even indoors.

Will there be tickets sold at the door?

No. Ticket sales end on April 11, the day before the event starts. It will not be possible to buy a ticket at the door.

I'm a member of the media, how do I get accreditation?

We treat everyone the same, and do not give out media passes. You are more than welcome to join us as a co-creator, by buying a membership (ticket) and participate fully in co-creating the event! For other media inquiries, please contact Gustaf Josefsson, 0709-854979.

Can we use candles and such?

Last year we had a super strict policy of no candles, no incense, no sage or burning of anything inside. I recommend you keep the same policy. Nightclub fire regulations are not really taking into account that there may be tents and fabric inside (source: Hugi Asgeirsson)

The 10 Principles

The Eclectical Spectacle builds its community by trying to find shared values, a way of understanding ourselves, what we value and how we do things together. These values are expressed in the 10 Principles of created for the global Burning Man community. They are not meant to be interpreted as "rules", but rather inspiration for deeper philosophical exploration.

Communal Effort

Our community values creative cooperation and collaboration. We strive to produce, promote and protect social networks, public spaces, works of art, and methods of communication that support such interaction.

Gifting

We are devoted to acts of gift giving. The value of a gift is unconditional. Gifting does not contemplate a return or an exchange for something of equal value.

Radical Self-expression

Radical self-expression arises from the unique gifts of the individual. No one other than the individual or a collaborating group can determine its content. It is offered as a gift to others. In this spirit, the giver should respect the rights and liberties of the recipient.

Civic Responsibility

We value civil society. Community members who organize events should assume responsibility for public welfare and endeavor to communicate civic responsibilities to participants. They must also assume responsibility for conducting events in accordance with local, state and federal laws.

Radical Self-reliance

We encourage the individual to discover, exercise and rely on his or her inner resources.

Leaving No Trace

Our community respects the environment. We are committed to leaving no physical trace of our activities wherever we gather. We clean up after ourselves and endeavor, whenever possible, to leave such places in a better state than when we found them.

Radical Inclusion

Anyone may be a part of our culture. We welcome and respect the stranger. No prerequisites exist for participation in our community.

Decommodification

In order to preserve the spirit of gifting, our community seeks to create social environments that are unmediated by commercial sponsorships, transactions, or advertising. We stand ready to protect our culture from such exploitation. We resist the substitution of consumption for participatory experience.

Immediacy

Immediate experience is, in many ways, the most important touchstone of value in our culture. We seek to overcome barriers that stand between us and a recognition of our inner selves, the reality of those around us, participation in society, and contact with a natural world exceeding human powers. No idea can substitute for this experience.

Participation

Our community is committed to a radically participatory ethic. We believe that transformative change, whether in the individual or in society, can occur only through the medium of deeply personal participation. We achieve being through doing. Everyone is invited to work. Everyone is invited to play. We make the world real through actions that open the heart.
